A New Zealand couple spent $14,000 creating a submarine-themed tiny home out of an old grain silo, hot tub, and spare parts

The idea to build the unusual tiny home came to Keith Lovelock, a retired radio technician, in 2016 when he was sitting in a yellow cafe. Six months later, his wife Jen Lovelock helped him put the idea into motion. While browsing Trade Me, an online auction site, Jen found an old fiberglass grain silo listed for $1. Intrigued, the pair hired a local company to pick up the silo and transport it to their farm in Marton for around $230.
